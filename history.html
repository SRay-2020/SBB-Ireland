History

SeeBeyondBorders was founded in 2009 by Edward Shuttleworth ,and his wife Kate, a primary school teacher. The organisation was founded on the belief that change begins with education and that good quality schooling allows Cambodian children to seek a brighter future and overcome the clutches of poverty. The Cambodian education system is still struggling to recover from the devastating impact of the Khmer Rouge regime when over ninety percent of teachers were killed. SeeBeyondBorders is a not-for-profit organisation that provides access to quality teaching and learning at school. We work in primary schools in Cambodia, focussing on early grades in primary schools.


In partnership with local communities we sustainably improve education standards by training teachers, getting children to school and developing better learning environments.We promote local participation and ownership across all of our programmes. In 2016 SeeBeyondBorders received the prestigious UNESCO prize for Outstanding Practice and Performance in Enhancing the Effectiveness of Teachers. SeeBeyondBorders comprises three legal entities; SeeBeyondBorders Australia, SeeBeyondBorders, UK and SeeBeyond Borders, Ireland. Each entity has its own board and adheres to local regulatory compliance requirements. Operations in each country are complementary and adhere to shared vision, mission and values.
